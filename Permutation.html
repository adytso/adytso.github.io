Permutation Recovery from Noisy Observations

Brief Overview

Consider a random vector \(X^n=X_1,…,X_n\) distributed i.i.d. according to some distribution \(P_X\), and consider the noisy observation \(Y^n=X^n+Z^n\) where \(Z^n\) is i.i.d. Gaussian noise. Classical estimation theoretic works seek to recover \(X^n\) from the noisy observation \(Y^n\). Suppose, instead, we are only interested in recovering the permutation of \(X^n\) (denoted by \(\pi\) ) instead of the value of \(X^n\). For example, if \(X^4=[ 3, 1, 7, 5]\), the permutation is \(\pi=[ 2, 1, 4,3]\) (i.e., ranking of values of \(X^4\)). This line of research seeks to understand the fundamental limits of recovering \(\pi\) (i.e., permutation of \(X^n\)) from the noisy observation \(Y^n\).
